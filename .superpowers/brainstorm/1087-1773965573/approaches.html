Three Approaches to the Redesign

Same goals: drive applications, feel premium. Different emphasis.

A

Proof-First Page

Cut the tagline section (§2). Replace the features grid with a live stats bar — season record, win rate, top ROI. Lock of the Day card moves up, shows full pick immediately. The page leads with evidence, not promises.

Cuts: §2 tagline. Reworks: §3 → stats bar, Lock card in §4 shows pick details. Adds: season record strip.

B

Trophy Moment Page

Collapse §2 into the hero. Replace §3 with a "Season So Far" trophy wall — biggest wins, current hot streak, best lock record. Lock of the Day gets its own hero-sized spotlight card at the top with pick details and a countdown to game time.

Cuts: §2. Reworks: §3 → trophy wall, Lock of the Day → standalone hero card. Adds: game countdown.

C

Minimal + Sharp Page

Cut both §2 and §3 entirely. Tighten the hero copy to be sharper and more exclusive. Let §4 (live activity) carry the whole page — but redesigned as a single live dashboard preview showing locks with full picks, leaderboard, and feed in one tight scrollable card. Less is more.

Cuts: §2 and §3 entirely. Reworks: §4 → unified dashboard preview card, hero copy tightened.

My pick: B — the trophy wall + Lock spotlight creates the biggest "I want in" moment for a visitor. But C is a strong option if you want the page to feel tight and minimal.
